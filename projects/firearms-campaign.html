Firearms Campaign for Deirdre Macnab

Client

Deirdre Macnab, head of the League of Women Voters of Florida, opposes to carry firearms on campus.

Problems

The bill, which would allow people to carry concealed-weapons on campus, failed to pass the Legislature during the 2015 session. However, the same bill has been filed to be considered during the 2016 legislative session. The League of Women Voters of Florida needs more publics’ and organizations’ supports to against the bill in 2016.

Desired Outcome

To prevent the campus-carry bills (SB 68 and HB 4001) from passing the legislature during the 2016 session.

Target Audience

  • Primary audience: Students, University Staffs, Parents, University Police
  • Secondary audience: Media, Local Government, Local Organizations

Objectives

  • Acknowledge the primary audience that allow to carry firearms on campus is dangerous. li>
  • Dismiss the supports for campus-carry bills.

Public Relations Planning

Formative Research

  1. Situation
    • 8 states now allow the carrying of concealed weapons on public postsecondary campuses; 19 states ban concealed weapons on campus; 23 states leave the decision up to individual colleges.
    • In Florida, all university presidents and campus law enforcement, United Faculty of Florida, Faculty Senates, Student Government Associations, campus student groups, the League of Women Voters of Florida, and community and faith leaders oppose firearms on campuses bills.
    • A recent study conducted by the Campaign to Keep Guns off Campus using FBI Uniform Crime Statistics and Clery Act data from 2004-2013 shows that the presence of concealed weapons on campus does not cause a drop in crime.
    • If campus-carry bills are signed into law, it will present a significant financial burden to all schools at an estimated cost of over $50 million dollars.
  2. Publics
    • Qualitative research: Organize focus groups between students and staff in different universities located in Florida to describe the options and reasons related to the campus-carry bill, such as why they support or against the bill.
    • Quantitative research: Create different online surveys for students, University staffs, parents and polices in different universities separately, and collect all the survey. Try to understand their stance on these bills, and their reasons such as why they support or against the bills. The survey is delivered through e-mail.

Strategies

  1. Utilize news release, fact sheet, brochures, videos, pictures and films to strengthen information dissemination.
  2. Utilize social media that print-like one-way communication to reveal more attention, and disseminate on social media platform like two-way communication, such as Facebook and twitter.

Tactics

  1. Multimedia
    • Make microfilms about the danger of carrying guns on campus, and show it on social media, such as YouTube.
    • Make public service advertising or interview videos from parents, students, faculties, and university police to describe their reason for opposing bills in several directions. Broadcast these videos on television, and appeal or hire people to forward the video on social media.
    • Send emails to students in universities about the risk to carry guns on campus.
    • Publish news release and fact sheets on newspapers and magazines to announce the threat of carrying firearms on campus.
  2. Other Events
    • Ask organizations to organize petitions for opposing to carry guns on campus.
    • Giving out the brochures about opposing campus-carry bills on campus.
    • Invite leaders of organizations and the governor who against campus-carry bills to do speeches on campus.

Evaluating the Strategic Plan

  1. For Viability
    • How many people can receive the information we offer in our plan.
    • Which channel for the plan is most influential?
  2. For results
    • How many people change their attitude after the plan?
    • For the primary audience, who mostly change after the plan?
    • Which reason most impact you if you change your attitude?
    • How many people would like to disseminate the information in our plan to others?
